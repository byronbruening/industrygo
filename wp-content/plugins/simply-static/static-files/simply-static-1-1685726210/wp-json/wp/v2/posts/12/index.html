Coromandel Express train from Kolkata to Chennai in India suffered a terrible accident. The accident took place in Balasore, Odisha state, 250 km south of Kolkata, around 7:30 pm today. Although more than two hundred people were injured, it is not known whether anyone died or not.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Odisha Chief Secretary Pradeep Jena told reporters at around 9:30 pm that the collision took place between the Coromandel Express, a local train and a freight train. Several coaches were derailed and many were damaged. About 60 ambulances and more than 15 units of the fire brigade were dispatched to the scene.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee said that several people from Kolkata are in danger of being on this train and there is a danger of being injured. The Coromandel Express train was traveling from Kolkata’s Shalimar station to Chennai, the capital of Tamil Nadu in South India. Many people from Kolkata go to Chennai for treatment by this train.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mamata Banerjee said that a team of 5 or 6 people is being sent to Odisha from West Bengal. He announced two emergency numbers for inquiries saying he was in touch with the Odisha government. These two numbers are 0091-33-22143526 and 0091-33-22535185.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
